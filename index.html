Tazhib

Tazhib is a Persian word which refers to the art of illuminating and decorating manuscripts and books with intricate designs, patterns, and calligraphy. This art form has a long history in Iran and the Ottoman Empire, and was used to embellish religious texts, poetry, and historical chronicles. Tazhib involves the creation of intricate geometric and floral patterns using only the hand, without the aid of computers. The focus of tazhib is on achieving balance and symmetry in the patterns, and the artist must have a keen eye for detail and a steady hand to create these masterpieces. The beauty of Tazhib lies in its simplicity and the unique handcrafted quality that each piece possesses.

Line Drawing

Line art, also known as line drawing, is a style of art that consists solely of lines and is defined by the use of continuous or broken lines to create shapes, patterns, and images. Line art can be created using a variety of mediums, including pencil, pen, marker, or ink. This style of art is often used to create simple, yet striking, images, and is characterized by a lack of shading or texture. Line art is often used in graphic design, illustration, and animation, and can be used to create logos, icons, and illustrations for products, books, and websites. Line art is also a popular choice for coloring books, as it provides a clear and simple outline for coloring. In wiki, line art is a versatile and accessible form of art that can be used to create a wide range of images and designs.

Doodling

Doodling is the act of drawing or sketching absentmindedly, often while talking on the phone or during a boring lecture. Doodles can range from simple shapes and patterns to more complex illustrations and can be created with any writing or drawing instrument, such as a pencil, pen, or marker. Doodling is often seen as a form of relaxation and creative expression, and can be a fun and creative outlet for people of all ages. Some people also see doodling as a way to tap into their unconscious mind and bring forth hidden thoughts or ideas. While doodles are usually thought of as spontaneous and unplanned drawings, they can also be a starting point for more serious drawings or paintings. Overall, doodling is a fun and easy way to get in touch with your creative side and pass the time.

I have found that the art of line drawing and doodling not only allows me to tap into my creative side, but also serves as a form of meditation and relaxation. These simple yet striking styles offer a welcome escape from the stresses of daily life and provide a calming outlet for my thoughts and emotions. I fully enjoy the process of focusing on lines and shapes. The act of drawing, whether I am drawing with a pencil or pen, has the power to soothe the mind and bring peace to the soul.
